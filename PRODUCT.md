# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Primary users are forensic and financial auditors reviewing a company dossier (for example the Muster Verpackungen exercise). Secondary users are in-house accounting and finance teams investigating irregularities in their own books. Both arrive mid-investigation: they need integrity they can trust, findings they can verify, and a path to a handoff-ready report.

## Product Purpose

Cortea is an offline-first forensic accounting workbench. It ingests a sealed dossier, verifies source exports, runs deterministic controls, and lets reviewers inspect findings against exact source rows, sheets, or pages. Optional AI assistance is citation-checked and never authoritative over the controls.

Success is confidence that the dossier’s source data and controls are trustworthy — with findings reviewed and an exportable report when the session is complete.

## Positioning

A workbench that turns a sealed dossier into reviewed findings and an exportable report.

## Brand Personality

Precise · Calm · Evidence-led. The interface should feel like an instrument panel: quiet chrome, clear status, no celebration. Voice is direct and technical without theatrics.

## Anti-references

- Flashy SaaS dashboards (gradient hero metrics, celebratory toasts, purple glow)
- Consumer fintech apps (playful cards, emoji, soft rounded “delight”)
- Dense legacy ERP screens (everything visible at once, no hierarchy)

## Design Principles

1. Evidence over ornament — every claim must open to a source.
2. Deterministic controls stay authoritative; assistance never outranks them.
3. Instrument calm — quiet chrome so judgment stays on the numbers.
4. One job per surface — verify, investigate, or export; don’t mix the modes.
5. Trust is the outcome — the UI should make integrity legible, not theatrical.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Prefer reduced-motion-safe transitions and status encoding that is not color-only where practical.
